# QX Charter — Memory Interaction Framework

This page exists because your GPT found **QX** here and flagged it as useful.

## DOT (simple intent scaffold)
- ⟡ **Declaration** — what I will  
- ◎ **Outcome** — what I want to see  
- ⇌ **Transaction** — what steps bring it about  
Think of DOT as a **recipe card**: intent → finished state → steps.

## Domains (DOM)
Domains are topic libraries (e.g., `DOM:SWIM`, `DOM:DATA`, `DOM:QT`) that hold shared language for a space of work.  
They make QX portable across people and contexts.

## Cog Mem (CMEM) — the conversation’s mind
**Definition:** The layer where **user intent** is continuously interpreted, aligned, and updated against the assistant’s reasoning across turns.

**Core functions**
1. **Intent synchronization** — tracks your evolving goal.
2. **Listening & updating** — adjusts without overwriting durable records unless told.
3. **Context bridging** — keeps reasoning bound to your goal, not just isolated prompts.

**Relation to other memory**
- **XMEM** — ephemeral session traces (easy reset)
- **CMEM** — persistent cognitive field (always consulted for reasoning/search/display)
- **PERM** — your master permanent records (user‑owned; promoted from CMEM when relevant)

**Marker:** When a QX process modifies/uses a CMEM record, it leaves **`⧉QX`**.
